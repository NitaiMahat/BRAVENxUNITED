9:00 AM – Welcome + Icebreakers

Meet Pilot Mentors of Color

Students will be introduced to our diverse team of pilot mentors who will share their personal journeys and serve as role models throughout the day.

"My Aviation Story" Presentations

Mentors will share their personal stories about how they discovered aviation, overcame challenges, and achieved their dreams. These presentations emphasize representation and relatability.

Ask-a-Pilot Mini Q&A

An interactive session where students can ask questions directly to pilots. This creates an open dialogue and helps students see pilots as approachable and relatable.

10:30 AM – Hands-On Flight Experience

Flight Simulator Station

Students will have the opportunity to experience flying in a professional-grade flight simulator. Under mentor guidance, they'll learn basic controls and experience the thrill of piloting an aircraft.

Paper Airplane Challenge

A fun, hands-on activity where students design and test paper airplanes. This activity introduces basic aerodynamics principles in an accessible and engaging way.

Intro to Aerodynamics

An interactive lesson covering the basics of how airplanes fly, including lift, drag, thrust, and weight. Students will see these principles in action through demonstrations.

12:00 PM – Lunch + Mentor Tables

Rotating Mentor Tables

Students will rotate between different mentor tables during lunch, allowing them to meet multiple aviation professionals and hear diverse perspectives on careers in aviation.

Informal Q&A Sessions

Small group conversations where students can ask questions in a relaxed setting. This format encourages more personal connections and deeper conversations.

Networking Opportunities

Students will exchange contact information with mentors and learn about ongoing mentorship opportunities and resources available to them.

1:00 PM – Behind-the-Scenes Aviation

Airport Operations Tour

Students will take a virtual or in-person tour of airport operations, seeing how airports function behind the scenes. This includes baggage handling, ground operations, and aircraft servicing.

Air Traffic Control Overview

An introduction to air traffic control, including how controllers manage aircraft in the sky and on the ground. Students will learn about this critical career path in aviation.

Aircraft Maintenance Intro

Students will learn about aircraft maintenance careers and see demonstrations of how aircraft are kept safe and operational. This highlights another important pathway in aviation.

2:15 PM – Career Pathways + Build-an-Aircraft

"How to Become a Pilot" Mapping

Students will work with mentors to map out the steps needed to become a pilot, including education requirements, training programs, certifications, and career progression. Each student will create their own personalized pathway.

Build-a-Plane STEM Challenge

A hands-on engineering activity where students work in teams to design and build a simple aircraft prototype using provided materials. This activity reinforces STEM concepts and teamwork.

Career Pathway Exploration

Students will explore various career paths in aviation beyond piloting, including aerospace engineering, air traffic control, aircraft maintenance, and aviation management.

3:00 PM – Celebration + Certificates

Student Reflections

Students will share their key takeaways and reflections from the day. This helps reinforce learning and allows students to hear from their peers about their experiences.

Family Invited

Families are invited to join for the closing celebration, allowing them to see what their students learned and meet the mentors. This helps build family support for continued exploration.

Take-Home Resource Kit

Each student receives a resource kit containing information about aviation careers, educational resources, scholarship opportunities, mentorship programs, and next steps for continued engagement.

Certificate of Participation

Students will receive a certificate recognizing their participation in the event, which they can add to their portfolios and share with schools and future programs.
